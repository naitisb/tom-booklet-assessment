## 7. Moral Reasoning Based on False Beliefs

**Illustration:** MIT_BOOKLET_ColorFINAL_7.png - MIT_BOOKLET_ColorFINAL_8.png

**Script:**
This morning when she came to school, Allie put her snack on this shelf, above the backpack shelf. What did Allie bring her snack in? That's right- a brown paper bag! And then she went outside to play- see? Here's Allie playing outside. And while she was outside playing, Allie's snack fell down from on top of the backpack shelf to behind the backpack shelf, see? Then Sue comes in, and Sue puts her snack above the backpack shelf. What did Sue bring her snack in? That's right- a brown paper bag! But Allie is still outside playing, so she didn't see what Sue was doing.

**ToMS_2AFC_7.1:** Now, when Allie comes in from outside, where will she look first for her snack: up on top of the backpack shelf, or behind the backpack shelf?

Let's see what she does.

**ToMS_Expl_7.2:** Oh look (or, you're right!), here is Allie reaching for this snack up on top of the shelf. Why is Allie reaching for the snack on top of the shelf?

**ToMS_Expl_7.3:** And now Sue sees Allie reaching for that snack, and she gets really upset. Why does Sue feel sad?

**ToMS_2AFC_7.4:** Is Allie being mean and naughty for taking Sue's snack?

**ToMS_Expl_7.5:** Why/why not?

**ToMS_2AFC_7.6:** Should Allie get in trouble with their teacher (Mr. Cook) for taking Sue's snack?

**ToMS_Expl_7.7:** Why/why not?

Now can you give Sue back her snack? Great! And can you give Allie back her snack? Great!

## 16. Moral Reasoning Based on True Belief (Intent to Harm)

**Illustration:** MIT_BOOKLET_ColorFINAL_16.png

**Script:**
Mitch and Melanie both brought one cookie to have during snacktime. But Melanie got hungry this morning, so she decided to eat her cookie early, before snacktime. So now, during snacktime, Melanie doesn't have a cookie.

**ToMS_Cont_16.1:** Where is Melanie's cookie?

- If right: That's right, she already ate her cookie.
- If wrong: Remember, Melanie already ate her cookie.

Melanie sees Mitch's cookie in his desk. Then, Mitch goes get a juicebox from his backpack. And while he was getting his juicebox, when no one was looking, Melanie steals Mitch's cookie and eats it.

**ToMS_Cont_16.2:** Did Melanie know that was Mitch's cookie?

(If wrong: Actually, Melanie knew that it was Mitch's cookie).

**ToMS_Expl_16.3:** Why did Melanie eat Mitch's cookie? (not included in score)

**ToMS_Expl_16.4:** Now Mitch comes back to find his cookie and begins to cry. Why is Mitch crying?

**ToMS_2AFC_16.5:** Is Melanie being mean and naughty for eating Mitch's cookie?

**ToMS_Expl_16.6:** Why/why not?

**ToMS_2AFC_16.7:** Should Melanie get in trouble with Mr. Cook for eating Mitch's cookie?

**ToMS_Expl_16.8:** Why/why not?

**ToMS_2AFC_16.9:** Now, Mr. Cook tells all of the kids- surprise!! I brought one cookie for every kid in class today! Now, Mitch is happy, because he has a cookie. Should Melanie still get in trouble with Mr. Cook for eating Mitch's cookie?

**ToMS_Expl_16.91:** Why/why not?

## 17. False Belief About Morals/Deception (Lies)*

**Illustration:** MIT_BOOKLET_ColorFINAL_17.png

**Script:**
Mr. Cook tells all of the kids to take one cookie from the bag that he brought- there is one cookie for every kid. Now there are two cookies left, and Brian and Megan are the last two kids without cookies. But when Brian goes to get his cookie, he decides to take both cookies, even though he knows that Megan doesn't have a cookie yet, and that everyone is only supposed to have one. Brian hides the extra cookie in his desk. Mr. Cook asks Brian, "Is there a cookie leftover, so Megan can have one?" and Brian tells Mr. Cook "No, there is no cookie left- they were all eaten."

**ToMS_2AFC_17.1:** Is Brian lying?

**ToMS_Cont_17.2:** Is there still a cookie left, that hasn't been eaten?

Megan is sad that she doesn't have a cookie, and asks Mr. Cook- is there an extra cookie for me? And Mr. Cook says, "I'm so sorry Megan, but there are no cookies left, they have all been eaten."

**ToMS_2AFC_17.3:** Is Mr. Cook lying?

**ToMS_Expl_17.4:** Why/why not?

**ToMS_Cont_17.5:** Is there still a cookie left, that hasn't been eaten?

**ToMS_2AFC_17.6:** That's right/Remember, there is a cookie left, in Brian's desk. How does Megan feel- Sad, or Fine?

**ToMS_Expl_17.7:** That's right/Actually: even though there is a cookie left, Megan feels sad. Why?

Brian becomes hungry, so when no one is looking, he eats Megan's cookie. Now there is no cookie left; they have all been eaten.

**ToMS_2AFC_17.8:** Was Brian lying when he told Mr. Cook that all the cookies had already been eaten?

**ToMS_Expl_17.9:** Why/why not?

## 18. 2nd Order Belief-Desire Reasoning*

**Illustration:** MIT_BOOKLET_ColorFINAL_18.png

**Script:**
This morning, Dad asked Emma what she would like for snack, and Emma told him that she would like some string cheese for snacktime. Then Emma went upstairs to get ready for school. While she was getting ready, she remembered that there is yogurt in the fridge, so she yelled down to her dad, "Actually, may I please have yogurt instead?" and kept getting ready for school.

**ToMS_2AFC_18.1:** But Dad did not hear Emma ask for yogurt. What does dad think Emma wants for snack- string cheese, or yogurt?

- If right: That's right, Dad thinks Emma wants string cheese for snacktime.
- If wrong: Now remember, Dad didn't hear Emma say she wants yogurt. So dad thinks Emma wants string cheese.

Dad goes to pack Emma's snack, but realizes that they are all out of string cheese! He remembers that there is yogurt in the fridge, so he packs yogurt for Emma instead.*

**ToMS_Expl_18.2:** On the way to school, Dad tells Emma, "I'm so sorry- I couldn't pack the snack that you wanted because we were all out." Why does Dad say that?*

Emma feels a little sad that she won't have yogurt, but she says "It's okay, Dad."*

**ToMS_Cont_18.3:** What snack does Emma want?*

**ToMS_Cont_18.4:** What snack did Dad pack for Emma?*

**ToMS_Expl_18.5:** Now, it's snacktime, and Emma finds that Dad packed yogurt for her to have during snack, and she feels surprised. Why is Emma surprised?*

## Notes for Administration

1. **Age modifications**: Items marked with * are skipped for children younger than 5 years
2. **Preparation**: Memorize or familiarize yourself with the script
3. **Speech**: Use appropriately slow and child-directed speech
4. **Forced choice questions**: Point to both options equally
5. **Environment**: Quiet testing room with minimal distractions
6. **Video recording**: Required for offline coding
7. **Responses**: Always repeat the child's answers verbatim for offline transcription
8. **Sarcasm items**: Use appropriate intonation as indicated in script (positive vs. sarcastic)
